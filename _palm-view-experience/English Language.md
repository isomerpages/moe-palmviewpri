<h2>ENGLISH LANGUAGE</h2>
<p>The department believes in building a strong foundation in effective language
use and communication for all our students. We adopt a systematic approach
to the teaching of language skills. Teachers make use of rich texts and
a variety of platforms to develop and strengthen language proficiency.</p>
<h4>Pedagogy</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width:90%" height="auto" width="100%" src="/images/Students engaged in STELLAR story time.jpg">
</div>
<p>Students engaged in STELLAR story time.</p>
<p>The core features of our EL classrooms include: STELLAR (Strategies for
the Teaching of English Language and Reading), shared book approach, explicit
teaching of vocabulary and grammar, modified language experience approach
and learning centres.&nbsp;</p>
<p>To encourage and support our students in their writing, we make use of
the Picture Word Induction Method (PWIM). This is a multifaceted approach
which allows students to “increase sight-word and writing vocabularies,
phonetic and structural analysis, and use observation and analysis in their
study of reading and writing” (Calhoun, 1999). Through careful selection
of pictures which are aligned to the books covered within STELLAR, students
build on their vocabulary skills by making meaningful connections between
aural and written words.&nbsp;To build a strong foundation in the other
language areas, our teachers design specific lessons, activities and packages
that emphasise on introducing, reinforcing, applying and extending the
learning of concepts and skills. This allows for a comprehensive and in-depth
learning of the English Language.</p>
<h4>Media Resource Library</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width:100%" height="auto" width="100%" src="/images/Students spending quality time in the Media Resource Library.jpg">
</div>
<p>Students spending quality time in the Media Resource Library</p>
<p>Our library is a great place of learning for the students. Our department
teachers work closely with the various departments to facilitate the many
activities conducted at the library. The activities range from whole school
to specific learning groups. Teachers come up with creative themes and
transform the library into vibrant learning spaces for the students. We
work closely with our parent volunteers to carry out activities like Reading
Mums, games and storytelling sessions to increase our students’ love for
reading. Our teachers take their students into the library to conduct lessons
and activities. The library is equipped with books of varied genres and
facilitates bulk borrowing to ensure that every student is provided with
opportunities to read within and beyond the classroom.</p>
<h4>English and Mathematics Carnival</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width:100%" height="auto" width="100%" src="/images/Students participating in a story telling competition.png">
</div>
<p>Students participating in the Once Upon a Tale Story Telling Competition
during EL Fest</p>
<p>Our annual English Learning Festival encourages students to explore the
wonders of the English language through a series of meaningful and exciting
events.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Starting from 2020, we have collaborated with the Mathematics department
in this event. Through this meaningful collaboration, we aim to enhance
student engagement in making the learning of English and Mathematics fun
both in and out of the classroom, to learn through authentic experiences,
strengthen students’ understanding of concepts and encourage greater appreciation
and interest in both English and Mathematics.&nbsp;</p>
<p>During their English lessons, students built on their writing and speaking
skills through a variety of activities. Primary 1 students were involved
in a Show and Tell session in which they had fun sharing with their classmates
about the jungle animal masks that they had made. Primary 2 and 3 students
got their creative juices flowing by creating their own fractured fairy
tales. The Primary 4 to 6 students explored different forms of creative
expression such as poetry, drama and poster design.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Students enjoyed an insightful assembly programme which highlighted the
differences between Singlish and English. Students also participated in
the Once Upon a Tale competition, which is our school’s first ever level
wide story-telling contest.&nbsp; &nbsp;</p>
<p>Carnival-themed recess booths were set up and many students enjoyed playing
the games during recess. The library was also abuzz with excitement as
students participated in the DIY Mr Men recess activity and the Library
Naming Competition.&nbsp;</p>
<p>We hope that with the engaging learning experiences offered during this
festival, our students will develop a greater appreciation and love for
the English Language.</p>
<h4><strong>Little Theatre</strong></h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Little_Theatre.jpg">
</div>
<p>Our “Little Theatre” programme introduces Primary 1 and 2 students to
the world of speech and drama in a fun and engaging way. Through a variety
of interactive activities, our students develop their voice projection,
expression and confidence while learning to communicate effectively. They
also build important skills such as teamwork, creativity and self-expression
as they take on different roles and bring stories to life. The programme
provides a supportive platform for our young learners to grow in confidence
and enjoy performing in front of an audience.</p>
<h4><strong>Talent Management</strong></h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Debate_Competition.jpg">
</div>
<p>Our Talent Management programme nurtures students with a strong interest
and aptitude in English, preparing them to represent the school in external
English-related competitions. Through targeted training and enriched learning
experiences, our students develop their skills in areas such as public
speaking, storytelling and debate. While learning to express their ideas
clearly and confidently, they are guided to refine their techniques, respond
thoughtfully to different tasks and perform effectively in competitive
settings. Through these opportunities, our students grow as confident communicators
and are empowered to excel beyond the classroom.</p>
