<h1>CCE Curriculum</h1>
<p><strong>Vision</strong>
</p>
<p>Curious Minds, Caring Hearts, Creative Spirits</p>
<p><strong>Mission</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>To develop the whole child to be a force for good in the world.</p>
<p><strong>CCE (FTGP)/ CCE (MTL)</strong>
</p>
<p>CCE Lessons in primary schools comprise CCE (Form Teacher Guidance Period)
or CCE (FTGP) and CCE (Mother Tongue Language) or CCE (MTL). CCE (FTGP)
lessons involve the explicit teaching of values, social and emotional skills
and citizenship dispositions. The lessons also equip students with knowledge
and skills to better understand and navigate the real-world through the
six CCE content areas.</p>
<p>CCE (MTL) lessons aim to deepen the learning of moral values through cultural
stories, songs, proverbs, practices unique to each MTL. The lessons deepen
moral values and one's cultural identity.</p>

<p><strong>PAL</strong>
</p>
<p>At Primary 1 and 2 levels, Programme for Active Learning (PAL) offers
hands-on and experiential learning through the domains of Drama, Outdoor
Education, Visual Arts and Sports and Games. It provides opportunities
for students to discover new interests, develop character and enable them
to cultvate social-emotional competencies i.e. help-seeking, interpersonal
communication, positivity, self-reflection and more.</p>

<p><strong>Social Studies</strong>
</p>
<p>The Social Studies curriculum aims to shape students into informed, concerned
and participative citizens who appreciate the interconnectedness of Singapore
and the world by exploring thought-provoking issues. By fostering critical
thinking and reflective skills, the program deepens students' understanding
of key societal challenges both locally and globally while nurturing a
sense of belonging to the nation. Through inquiry-based learning, students
acquire the knowledge, skills and values needed to contribute responsibly
to society.</p>
